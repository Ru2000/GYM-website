The definition of this training is stated as "constantly varied functional movement performed at high intensity" what this basically means is that the workouts are function in nature, real life movements which are mimicked in the gym and a movement that helps move a heavy load through a long distance in the shortest time. Constantly varied as in the workouts are different every day and target a different energy system and body part so to shock the body continuously to give better results. High intensity is basically the total power output of the workout.

We also have something called as travel WODs which is basically for all the propel travelling for work and holiday and do not have access to a gym. It's all based in free hand and body weight movements. Just carry a skipping rope with you.

The regular class lasts for about an hour. Some people usually come in a little bit early to get some extra warm up and mobility done. How ever it's not a compulsion for you to come early. The class starts sharp at the hour and the first 10 mins are kept aside for stretching and warming up. The next 10mins are for explaining Te WOD and warming up for the main WOD. Usually we start with 10-15 mins of stretch or skill work followed by some sort of high intensity interval Circut work. The workout finished off with 5mina of cool down while doing a dynamic breathing exercise and then clearing up the floor for the next class to come in.

This depends on your goal but to start off with I would suggest you train 5 days a week so that you are very familiar with the form and technique. We usually work from monday to Friday but once you have reached a level when you do not need my supervision you can come in for the open gym hours on the weekend. You can always do a 3 days on and 1 day off then again a 2 days on. Let your body speak to you, it will never lie.
